A Few Accomplishments

Here are a few milestone accomplishments and happenings in my life so far:

Married my Best Friend

I am blessed enough to call this beautiful woman, Alejandra, my wife. We met in the Edison Program at GE, and she was both my rock and my joy throughout our time in the program. Now, she continues to be that as we continue life's many adventures together. She's an extremely talented engineer that is currently a Project Manager for GE's Additive division. If you ever need a skilled communicator, a organized hard-worker, and an absolute joy to be around, call this wonderful woman up to today!

Completed the Edison Engineering Program

After three challenging and demanding years, I completed the Edison Engineer Development Program through GE Aviation. In the midst of this program, I covered mutliple facets of the business such as Supply Chain, Engine Performance, Test Engineering, and Business Analytics. Spending time working projects across these divisions taught me a ton about the various interactions taking place between each as well as the levers that are important to the focused work groups versus the intermediaries. Pictured is our team winning the Design Challenge during our "Engineering @ GE" Conference, placing an all-time Top 10 performing score with competiting scores coming from 30 previous years of the conference.

Earned a Masters in CS

As part of the Edison Program at GE, we had the ability to study for a Master's program in fields beneficial to GE. The one I chose to complete was a Master's in Computer Science, specializing in Data Science, from Boston University. The skills acquired during my Master's pair well with those gained during my undergrad studies, a Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering from North Carolina State University, in the manner that not only do I understand how to solve a real-world problem, but I can also apply probabilistic models around the situation to help choose the most optimal solution.

Coached Basketball at Multiple Levels

Basketball has been a passion as mine since before I can remember. Literally - there are videos of me shooting on a Little Tikes goal nonstop at 18 months old. I love it and dedicated the first 20 years of my life playing and learning the game. From this experience, I have been the assistant coach at two High School Varsity teams (Topsail, Fuquay-Varina), multiple travel and rec leagues, and even hosted a wide array of skills camps across ages 5-18 during my time as an undergrad. My main coaching mentors have been Bob Barlett, Ernie Hobbie, and Dante Calabria.
